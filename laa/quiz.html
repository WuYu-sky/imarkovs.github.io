Linear functions
Question 1: The function $f(x) = ax+b$ is linear.
  1. Yes
    Incorrect. $f$ is equal to a linear function $ax$ plus a constant $b$. Such a function (called affine) is not linear. It does not satisfy neither the superposition property nor homogeneity property. (Check this out.)
  2. No
    Correct!
Question 2: The function $f(a,b,c) = ax^2+bx+c$ is linear.
  1. Yes.
    Correct! Proof: $f(a,b,c) = \begin{bmatrix} x^2 & x & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a\\b\\c \end{bmatrix}$.
  2. No.
    Incorrect. If $f$ was a function of $x$, then it would be nonlinear (more specifically quadratic). However, $f$ is a function of $a,b,c$.
Question 3: The function $f(a,b,c) = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ b & c \end{bmatrix}$ is linear.
  1. Yes.
    Correct! Proof: $f(a,b,c) = \begin{bmatrix} 1&0\\0&0 \end{bmatrix} a + \begin{bmatrix} 0&1\\1&0 \end{bmatrix} b + \begin{bmatrix} 0&0\\0&1 \end{bmatrix}c$.
  2. No.
    Incorrect.

  1. Yes.

  2. No.

Linear system of equations
Question 1: An overdetermined system of equations has no solution.
  1. Yes
    Incorrect.
  2. No
    Correct! Here is an example of an overdetermined system of equations that has a solution: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} x = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$.
Question 2: If an underdetermined system of equations $Ax=b$ has a solution, then it has infinitely many solutions.
  1. Yes
    Correct! Proof: Let $x_\text{p}$ be a solution of the system. Since $Ax=b$ is underdetermined, $A$ has at least one more column than rows. Then, $A$ has at least one-dimensional kernel $\mathcal{N}$. Then, $x_\text{p}+v$, for any $v\in\mathcal{N}$ is also a solution. Since, there are infinitely many different vectors $v\in\mathcal{N}$, there are infinitely many solutions.
  2. No
    Incorrect.
Question 3: A square system has a unique solution.
  1. Yes
    Incorrect.
  2. No
    Correct! Here is a counter example: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} x = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ has no solution.
