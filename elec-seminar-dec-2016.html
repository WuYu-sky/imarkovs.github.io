ELEC seminar, December 13, 14:00–16:00

Place: Building K, 7th floor

Format: 5-minute presentation for PhD students, 10-minute presentation for postdocs

Program

  1. Maarten Schoukens, Data-driven nonlinear identification [abstract]

    My past research was focused on the identification of nonlinear block-oriented systems starting from the best linear approximation. Currently I focus on multiple aspects of nonlinear system identification:
    1. extending the flexibility of block-oriented representations
    2. considering more general noise frameworks (instead of the standard additive output noise case)
    3. analyze the impact of model errors on nonlinear system identification problems
    4. going towards the modeling of nonlinear systems in a network (or interconnected) framework
    5. benchmarking of nonlinear system identification methods

  2. Koen Tiels, A nonlinear state-space toolbox for Matlab [abstract]

    A polynomial nonlinear state-space (PNLSS) model can capture many nonlinear dynamic behaviors and has been successfully applied in a large range of applications. The model structure can easily deal with multiple input multiple output (MIMO) systems. I will present PNLSS 1.0, a toolbox to estimate and simulate PNLSS models with Matlab.

  3. Jan Decuyper, PNLSS modelling of vortex-induced vibrations

  4. Alireza Fakhrizadeh Esfahani, NL SI and parameter reduction applied to a friction model: The Bouc Wen model [abstract]

    NL state space identification is very interesting specially for highly nonlinear systems. The polynomial nonlinear state space identification is used to fully identify the Bouc-Wen model, a friction nonlinear model. Tensor decomposition is applied to the fully identified model for reducing number of parameters.

  6. Mariya Ishteva, Overview of tensor methods

  7. Gabriel Hollander, An initialization method for nonlinear model reduction

  8. Péter Zoltán Csurcsia, A VLAIO Project: Development of an advanced time-varying and nonlinear nonparametric estimation framework for industrial measurements [abstract]

    The presentation briefly discusses the call for VLAIO (former IWT) Innovation Mandate applications and the first approved project of ELEC and Siemens Industry Software NV (SISW, former LMS). This project aims to transfer applicable knowledge from the department ELEC to SISW in two specific fields. The first one deals with nonparametric modal analysis of time-varying systems for Operational Modal Analysis (OMA). The second goal focuses on the detection and classification of system nonlinearities using nonparametric nonlinear analysis on general multiple-input, multiple-output (MIMO) systems. The basic concept of this project is to drastically reduce the necessary time needed for analysis and to transfer the knowledge to non-expert users.

  9. Rik Pintelon, An overview of the work related to time-varying system identification

  10. John Lataire,Time-varying systems, at the crossroads of system identification and machine learning [abstract]

    An overview of my research in time-varying systems will be presented. Topics include:
    • the appearance of skirts when applying a multisine to a linear time-varying system
    • non-parametric estimation of the time-varying transfer function by interpreting the skirts
    • parametric identification of time-varying ordinary differential equation (ODE)
    • the advantage of using kernel based regression in the context of the identification of time-varying ODEs.

  11. Anna Marconato, Filter-based regularization for impulse response modeling

  12. Georgius Birpoutsoukis, Volterra series estimation and regularization

  13. Julien Ertveldt, Application of frequency-domain system identification to wind tunnel experiments on the active aeroelastic test bench. [abstract]

    The recently developed Active Aeroelastic Test Bench allows to excite a rigid or cantilever wing from motion at its clamping point. Firstly, the reduction in measurement time based on the TV approach is studied from the measured input-output relations. Secondly, we evaluate the validity of linear modelling for increasing nonlinear behaviour of the wing at high angles of attack.

  14. Sandra Vásquez, Fault diagnosis on a hydraulic pitch system based on frequency domain identification [abstract]

    A model-based Fault Detection and Isolation (FDI) system is designed for the blade pitch system with hydraulic actuators, which is a critical subsystem of wind turbines. Two important issues have to be taken into account: the variation of the dynamics with the operating conditions (i.e wind speed and turbulence), and the need for active fault detection. We consider these factors by improving the frequency content of the pitch system’s excitation signal, and by identifying in the frequency domain a Linear Parameter-Varying model. Then, the model parameters are analyzed to diagnose faults, while the model uncertainty is used to provide robustness against noise and nonlinear distortions.

  15. Piet Bronders, Precompensation of supply modulated power amplifiers (SMPA) using a linear parameter-varying model (LPV) [abstract]

    A well-defined relationship between the modulated supply voltage and input power of a SMPA is crucial for obtaining an efficient linear device. Due to the inherent complexity of this relationship most of the current designs restrict themselves to a static non-linear framework. In my work I try to extend and/or modify this framework by also including the dynamic behaviour. A viable candidate for expanding the current state-of-art is, as I will discuss in this presentation, the use of an LPV model.

  16. Matthijs Van Berkel, Identification of the power dependency of electron transport in fusion devices [abstract]

    Scaling laws predict the performance of future enlarged fusion reactors such as DEMO. One of the key parameters in the scaling law is the heating power dependence (degradation of confinement). In this presentation, the relationship between heating power and radial distributed (turbulent) transport is investigated.

  17. Mussa Bshara, Behavioral modeling and digital pre-distortion of wideband power amplifiers. [abstract]

    My research focuses on the behavioral modeling and digital pre-distortion (DPD) of RF power amplifiers (PAs). As the demand of signal bandwidth continues to increase, the realization of both the PA and the DPD becomes more challenging to cope with the arising significant technical challenges. Those challenges include: wider bandwidths, multi-band operation, multi-protocol radios, higher frequency operation and realizing of high-efficiency PA architectures. Understanding the nonlinear behavior and dynamic properties of the PA will be a key enabler for the next-generation wideband networks, where efficient PA and DPD systems are required.
