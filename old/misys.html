Misys scholarships for ECS students

Summary

The Misys charitable foundation offers up to 5 scholarships per year to ECS students with strong information technology element in their study programme. All ECS students qualify, however, in the selection process advantage may be given to computer science majors. The scholarships are typically 1000 pounds for the academic year.

Eligibility conditions

  • Academic excellence, proven by at least "2:1" (preferably "First").
  • Financial need, proven by low parental income (less than 50K in total).
The scholarships are open to postgraduate and undergraduate, international and UK students. In the selection process, advantage is given to undergraduate students over postgraduate students and to UK students over international students. (See the selection procedure for details.)

Application procedure

  • Download and fill in the application form (doc file) and in (pdf file). Please note that the information for Section 9 should be filled in by the University.
  • Take a reference letter from your personal tutor (for the undergraduate students) or from the research supervisor (for the undergraduate students). This letter should comment on how well you conform with the selection criteria.
  • Submit the application form and the reference letter to Amanda Goodacre, room 2015, level 2 of Building 1, by 30 January.
  • You may be asked to be interviewed.
  • Successful candidates will be notified by email.

Selection procedure

The ECS responsible for the Misys scholarships is Ivan Markovsky.
  • Amanda filters the candidates that fail to satisfy the eligibility conditions and forwards the remaining application forms to Ivan. (Please note that it is your responsibility to provide sufficient information in the application form and where appropriate supporting documents.)
  • In February, Ivan shortlists up to 8 candidates and forwards their documents to the Misys charitable foundation for the final selection.
  • In May, the director of the Misys charitable foundation informs Ivan who are the successful candidates and Ivan notifies them by email.
Currently the shortlisting of the candidates is done in the following way. The UK undergraduate candidates having "First" are ranked according to their financial need and the first 8 are shortlisted. If there are less than 8 candidates in this group, international candidates, undergraduate candidates having "2:1" and postgraduate candidates are considered. The selection is done on the bases of the eligibility conditions and interviews.
